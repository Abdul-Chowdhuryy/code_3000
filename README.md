# AI Ethics Statement  
**Abdul Chowdhury**

Based on Chapter 5 of *Ethical Machines*, I believe that AI ethics statements should be practical and directly guide real-world decisions rather than exist as vague principles. Ethical AI must prioritize fairness, transparency, and accountability in ways that can be implemented and measured. It is not enough to claim that a system is unbiased; organizations must actively test, monitor, and improve their systems to reduce harm.

For example, in AI systems used for hiring, companies should regularly audit outcomes to ensure that certain groups are not being unfairly disadvantaged. This reflects the idea from the reading that ethics statements should influence actual behavior and decision-making processes, not just serve as public messaging.

I also believe organizations must take responsibility for the impact of their AI systems, especially in high-stakes areas such as healthcare, finance, and employment. Ethical principles should be supported by clear policies, oversight, and accountability mechanisms. Overall, AI should be developed in a way that respects individuals, minimizes harm, and builds trust through consistent and transparent practices.
